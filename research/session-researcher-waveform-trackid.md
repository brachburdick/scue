## Learnings
- Pioneer ANLZ detail waveforms (PWV5/PWV7) are 150 entries/second — higher temporal resolution than SCUE's current 60 FPS analysis. This makes Pioneer waveforms a viable immediate-display source while SCUE analysis runs in background.
- beat-link's TimeFinder depends on BeatGridFinder which depends on MetadataFinder — the entire position interpolation chain is broken on DLP hardware, not just metadata. SCUE must compute playback_position_ms entirely Python-side.
- CDJ-3000 sends precise position packets (200-byte status) but these are also accessed via TimeFinder, so the DLP workaround (beat_number + beatgrid) is still needed.
- PWV7 (3-band waveform in .2EX files) is the closest Pioneer equivalent to SCUE's RGB 3-band waveform. Only generated by CDJ-3000/XDJ-AZ/Opus Quad/recent rekordbox.
